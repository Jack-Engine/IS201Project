Career

The Journey of a Tennis Legend

Roger Federer is widely regarded as one of the greatest tennis players of all time. Born on August 8, 1981, in Basel, Switzerland, Federer turned professional in 1998 and went on to rewrite the history books with his elegance, athleticism, and unmatched sportsmanship.

Throughout his illustrious career, Federer won 20 Grand Slam singles titles, including a record eight Wimbledon championships, six Australian Opens, five US Opens, and one French Open. He held the World No. 1 ranking for a total of 310 weeks, including a record 237 consecutive weeks.

Federer played with a graceful one-handed backhand, incredible footwork, and mental toughness that inspired millions around the world. He represented Switzerland with pride, winning Olympic gold in doubles (2008) and silver in singles (2012), and led his country to a Davis Cup title in 2014.

Federer announced his retirement in 2022, leaving behind a legacy defined not just by victories, but by humility, elegance, and a love for the game. He continues to be involved in philanthropy through the Roger Federer Foundation, which supports educational projects in Southern Africa and Switzerland.
