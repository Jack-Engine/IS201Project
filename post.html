20 Grand Slam Titles

Federer’s journey through the most prestigious tennis tournaments

Posted by Federer Fan Club on April 23, 2025

Roger Federer’s name is etched into tennis history with an astounding 20 Grand Slam titles—a testament to his longevity, brilliance, and mastery of the sport. From his first Wimbledon triumph in 2003 to his final Grand Slam victory at the 2018 Australian Open, Federer dominated courts across the globe.

His achievements span all four major tournaments: 8 Wimbledon titles, 6 Australian Opens, 5 US Opens, and a solitary French Open title that completed his Career Grand Slam. Each title came with its own story—epic battles, emotional victories, and historic moments.

Consistency Across Surfaces

What sets Federer apart is not just the number of titles, but his ability to adapt and excel on all surfaces—grass, hard court, and clay. His 2009 French Open win, for example, was a breakthrough that silenced critics and proved his all-surface greatness.

“I always believed if you’re good enough, you can win anywhere.” — Roger Federer

Record Breaker and Fan Favorite

Federer became the first man to reach 20 Grand Slam titles, setting a benchmark for greatness. Even as rivals caught up, Federer’s elegance, humility, and crowd-favorite status remained unmatched.

Federer celebrates another milestone victory—an image etched into tennis history.

Though newer champions have emerged, Federer’s legacy of excellence and sportsmanship continues to inspire athletes and fans around the world.
