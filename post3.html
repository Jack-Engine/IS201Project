Wimbledon’s Greatest Champion

A look at Roger’s 8 Wimbledon titles and iconic moments

Posted by Federer Fan Club on April 23, 2025

Centre Court at Wimbledon is hallowed ground, and no one has walked it with more distinction than Roger Federer. With eight titles—the most in men’s history—Federer has become synonymous with Wimbledon elegance, dominance, and tradition.

From his first victory in 2003 to his record-breaking eighth in 2017, each championship run was filled with unforgettable rallies, poised performances, and historic matches.

A Court Fit for a King

Federer’s love affair with grass courts and the All England Club is the stuff of legend. His crisp white attire, the polite nods to the crowd, and his unmatched success have made him Wimbledon’s unofficial king.

“If you’re not nervous before Wimbledon, something’s wrong with you.” — Roger Federer
An iconic image: Federer holding the Wimbledon trophy aloft in triumph.

Wimbledon isn't just a tournament for Federer—it’s a second home. A place where grace meets greatness, and tradition meets talent.
